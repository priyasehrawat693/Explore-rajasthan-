Spiritual Rajasthan - Temples & Sacred Places

Discover divine architecture, ancient rituals, and spiritual peace in Rajasthan's magnificent temples

Temples of Rajasthan

1. Dilwara Jain Temples

Mount Abu
Dedicated to: Jain Tirthankaras

Considered Rajasthan's most beautiful Jain temple. Famous for exquisite white marble carvings with extreme fine detailing that appears translucent when sunlight passes through.

World Famous
Free Entry
Camera: ₹50-100

2. Brahma Temple

Pushkar (Ajmer)
Dedicated to: Lord Brahma

One of the world's rare Brahma temples. Located near Pushkar Lake, considered extremely sacred. Major pilgrimage site especially during Kartik Poornima.

World's Rare
Free Entry
Near Lake

3. Karni Mata Temple

Deshnok (Bikaner)
Dedicated to: Karni Mata

Famous as the "Rat Temple" housing thousands of rats considered sacred. It's believed that sighting a white rat brings good luck. Unique spiritual experience.

Rat Temple
Free Entry
Unique

4. Eklingji Temple

Udaipur (22 km from city)
Dedicated to: Lord Shiva

Royal deity of Mewar rulers. Ancient temple complex with peaceful atmosphere. Features a four-faced Shiva lingam made of black marble. Complex has 108 temples.

Royal Deity
Free Entry
Peaceful

5. Shri Nathji Temple

Nathdwara (Near Udaipur)
Dedicated to: Lord Krishna

Main temple of Pushti Marg sect. Extremely important for Krishna devotees. Famous for its "darshan" timings and rituals. The idol is believed to be self-manifested.

Krishna Bhakti
Free Entry
Timed Darshan

6. Govind Dev Ji Temple

Jaipur (City Palace area)
Dedicated to: Lord Krishna

Former royal temple of Jaipur's Kachwaha dynasty. Famous for its beautiful aartis and bhajans performed 7 times daily. Located in the heart of Jaipur.

Famous Aartis
Free Entry
Royal Temple

7. Birla Mandir Jaipur

Jaipur
Dedicated to: Lord Vishnu & Goddess Lakshmi

Modern white marble temple built by Birla family. Beautifully illuminated at night. Features carvings depicting Hindu mythology and modern science concepts.

White Marble
Free Entry
Night View

8. Mehandipur Balaji Temple

Dausa district
Dedicated to: Lord Hanuman (Balaji)

Famous for exorcism rituals and liberation from evil spirits. Unique rituals and ceremonies performed. People from all over India visit seeking relief from supernatural problems.

Exorcism
Free Entry
Unique Rituals

9. Ranakpur Jain Temple

Pali district (Near Udaipur)
Dedicated to: Jain Tirthankara Adinath

Architectural marvel with 1444 intricately carved marble pillars, no two alike. Built in 15th century. The temple changes color from golden to pale blue during the day.

1444 Pillars
₹50-100 Entry
Color Changing

10. Khatu Shyam Ji Temple

Khatu village, Sikar district
Dedicated to: Shyam Baba (Lord Krishna)

Kaliyug ke devta maane jaate hain. Famous for Phalgun Mela (Feb–March) when lakhs of devotees visit. Experience darshan + bhajan + kirtan. Morning & evening aarti times are best.

Lakhs of Devotees
Free Entry
Phalgun Mela

11. Salasar Balaji Temple

Salasar town, Churu district
Dedicated to: Lord Hanuman (Balaji)

Manokamna poori hone ke liye famous. Special belief: Khatu Shyam Ji ke saath yatra puri mani jaati hai. Major events: Chaitra Purnima & Ashvin Purnima mela. Pilgrims do pad-yatra too.

Wish Fulfillment
Free Entry
Pad-yatra

12. Osian Jain Temples

Osian (near Jodhpur)
Dedicated to: Jain Tirthankaras

Ancient desert-side temples dating back to 8th-11th centuries. Group of Hindu and Jain temples known as "Khajuraho of Rajasthan". Heritage + temple visit experience.

Ancient
Free/Minimal
Heritage Site

Temple Visit Guide

Best Time to Visit Temples

⏰ Morning: 6 AM - 9 AM (for aarti)

🌅 Evening: 5 PM - 7 PM (for evening prayers)

📅 Season: October to March (pleasant weather)

🌙 Full Moon: Special significance at many temples

Dress Code & Etiquette

👗 Clothing: Modest, covered shoulders & knees

👣 Footwear: Remove before entering temple

📵 Electronics: Phones often not allowed inside

💰 Donations: Optional but appreciated

Photography Rules

📷 General: Check signs or ask permission

🚫 Restrictions: No photography inside sanctum

💰 Fees: Camera fees ₹50-200 at some temples

📱 Mobile: Usually allowed for exterior shots

Spiritual Tourism in Rajasthan

Why Rajasthan for Spiritual Tourism?

Rajasthan offers a unique blend of spirituality through ancient temples, sacred lakes, and living traditions. The state is home to:

  • Diverse Faiths: Hindu, Jain, Muslim, Sikh places of worship
  • Ancient Temples: Some dating back to 6th-7th centuries
  • Sacred Lakes: Pushkar Lake, Nakki Lake, Pichola
  • Pilgrimage Circuits: Jain circuit, Hindu pilgrimage routes
  • Living Traditions: Daily rituals unchanged for centuries

Major Temple Festivals

Pushkar Camel Fair

Temple: Brahma Temple, Pushkar

When: November (Kartik Poornima)

Significance: Sacred bath in Pushkar Lake + world's largest camel fair

Maha Shivratri

Temples: Eklingji Temple, all Shiva temples

When: February/March

Significance: Night-long prayers and special pujas for Lord Shiva

Paryushan Parva

Temples: All Jain temples (Dilwara, Ranakpur)

When: August/September

Significance: Jain festival of forgiveness and introspection

Temple Circuit Planning Tips

📍 Jain Temple Circuit

• Day 1: Ranakpur Temple

• Day 2: Dilwara Temples (Mount Abu)

• Day 3: Jaisalmer Jain Temples

• Best time: October-March

🕉️ Hindu Temple Circuit

• Day 1: Eklingji Temple (Udaipur)

• Day 2: Nathdwara & Ghasiyar Mahadev

• Day 3: Pushkar Brahma Temple

• Include: Govind Dev Ji (Jaipur)

🎒 Travel Tips

• Check temple timings online

• Hire local guide for history

• Carry cash for donations

• Respect local customs
