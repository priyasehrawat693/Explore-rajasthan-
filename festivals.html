Rajasthani Festivals - Colors, Culture & Celebrations

Experience the vibrant festivals of Rajasthan that showcase its rich cultural heritage, traditions, and royal splendor

Major Festivals of Rajasthan

🐪 Pushkar Camel Fair Pushkar

October – November World Famous

One of the largest and most famous fairs in the world. Attracts tourists from across the globe with its vibrant display of Rajasthani culture, camel trading, and traditional competitions.

Camel Fair
World Famous
₹200-500

🌵 Desert Festival Jaisalmer

February Desert Culture

Celebrates the rich desert culture of Rajasthan against the golden sand dunes. A spectacular showcase of traditional music, dance, and desert sports in the Thar Desert.

Desert Festival
Folk Performances
₹200-500

🌸 Gangaur Festival Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur

March – April Women's Festival

Dedicated to Goddess Gauri, symbol of marital happiness and prosperity. Celebrated with great enthusiasm by women across Rajasthan with colorful processions and rituals.

Women's Festival
Religious
Free Entry

🌼 Teej Festival Jaipur & other cities

July – August (Monsoon) Women's Celebration

Celebrates the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, mainly celebrated by women. Features colorful processions, traditional swings, and women dressed in green attire.

Women's Festival
Monsoon Festival
Free Entry

🎭 Mewar Festival Udaipur

March – April Royal Festival

Marks the arrival of spring and showcases royal traditions of Udaipur. Features cultural performances near Lake Pichola, folk music, dances, and fireworks.

Royal Festival
Cultural Shows
₹200-500

🐘 Elephant Festival Jaipur

March (around Holi) Unique Festival

Displays the royal heritage of Jaipur with beautifully decorated elephants. Features elephant parades, polo matches, and traditional Rajasthani cultural performances.

Elephant Parade
Animal Sports
Mostly Free

Seasonal Festival Calendar

Plan your visit according to Rajasthan's festival seasons

Winter Festivals (Oct-Feb)

Pushkar Camel Fair, Desert Festival, Kite Festival

Best for: Cultural experiences, photography

Spring Festivals (Mar-Apr)

Gangaur, Mewar Festival, Elephant Festival, Holi

Best for: Colorful celebrations, traditional rituals

Monsoon Festivals (Jul-Aug)

Teej Festival, Raksha Bandhan celebrations

Best for: Women's festivals, religious ceremonies

🎨 Brij Holi Bharatpur Region

March Color Festival

Traditional celebration connected with Lord Krishna, celebrated with music and colors. Features unique Holi traditions specific to the Braj region of Rajasthan.

Color Festival
Folk Music
Free Entry

🪁 Kite Festival Jaipur

January (Makar Sankranti) Sky Festival

The sky of Jaipur becomes colorful with thousands of kites during this festival. A vibrant celebration marking the transition of sun into Capricorn.

Kite Flying
Food Festivals
Free Entry

🌞 Summer Festival Mount Abu

May–June Hill Station Festival

Celebrated in Rajasthan's only hill station, Mount Abu. A cool retreat with cultural performances, boat races, and traditional fairs during peak summer.

Hill Station
Boat Races
₹300-800

Festival Travel Guide & Tips

Planning Your Festival Visit

Make the most of your festival experience in Rajasthan with these essential tips:

  • Book Accommodation Early: Hotels fill up months in advance
  • Carry Cash: Many vendors don't accept cards
  • Dress Appropriately: Respect local customs and traditions
  • Stay Hydrated: Carry water during day events
  • Learn Basic Phrases: Simple Hindi greetings help

Festival Costs & Budget Planning

Entry Costs

Free Festivals: Gangaur, Teej, Kite Festival, Brij Holi

Paid Festivals: Pushkar Fair (₹200-500), Desert Festival (₹200-500)

Premium Events: Special shows at Mewar Festival (₹500-2000)

Elephant Festival: Mostly free, some paid seating

Accommodation Costs

Budget: ₹1000-2500/night (guesthouses)

Mid-range: ₹2500-6000/night (3-4 star hotels)

Luxury: ₹8000-20000+/night (heritage hotels)

Festival Season: Prices increase 50-100%

Food & Shopping

Street Food: ₹50-200 per meal

Restaurant: ₹300-800 per meal

Shopping: Handicrafts ₹500-5000

Local Transport: ₹500-1500 per day
